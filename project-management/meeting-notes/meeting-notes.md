## Notes
- Why are we doing this?
- Recognize that there is a label for your role
- Allow people to identify with and put name to their labour
- Allow people to decide what flavor of community manager are you
- Ensnare people in the web of RCM Cooperative...
- Awareness building about the role and definition
- Affirmation for valuable contribution - gives them confidence to do the good work

Pitch by Cass
I have a question for you. Please put your hand up (online emoji) if you do any of these things for a group of people which you may or may not call a “community”? 
Formally or informally (paid or not paid)

Do you:
Put on events (seminars, journal clubs, training, bigger things)
Promote your community internally or externally
Write or disseminate a newsletter about your community
Support to Onboarding or mentoring in your group
Write documentation for your community
Make connections/introductions between people
Work to make your community activities accessible and inclusive
Advocate for your community at leadership
Think about ways to bring new people into your community or support existing contributors
Try to listen to what your community needs or wants
Share things on slack or mailing lists?
Maybe you just bring the vibes!
If you do any of these things (any many more!), you are a community supporter!

Once you know you are a community supporter you can connect with other people who operate in similar contexts as you, and face the same challenges

When we are connected we can:
Share successes and challenges
Build a common language and frameworks
Develop our skills
Advocate for community support career paths
Make community support an open and reproducible methodology
Aim is to build a buzzfeed-style quiz to identify what type or “flavour” of community supporter you are

Doing this will help you
Recognise and “name” the essential support you provide (maybe make it visible…!)
Find other people with skills to support your communities 
Build an identity for people like you, and maybe a sticker for our lanyards!
Who should come
People who know they are community supporters
People who think they might be community supporters
Bring whatever skills you have - you are all incredibly skilled people - and lets see what we can build together!
